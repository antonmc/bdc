The link you just clicked on is from a fictitious email simulating a phishing attempt. Had this not been a simulation, sensitive, personal and organizational information would have been compromised. In today’s connected world, and given the nature of BDC’s business, it is imperative to remain vigilant at all times.
Don’t be the next victim of phishing – review these tips in detail!
When investigating the legitimacy of an email, always verify the sender’s email address and the links within the email.
1. Email address
This is the easiest and often most effective way to identify a phishing email. Check to see if the domain of the sender’s email address is consistent with a legitimate organization.

name@gouv.ca

Example:
name@gouv.ca | scammer@g0uv.ca

Don’t let a sub-domain fool you! Always look at the domain.

name@subdomain.domain.ca

Example:
name@gouv.ca | scammer@gouv.g0uv.com
2. Link embedded in the email
If the email contains a link, do not click on it until you’ve hovered your mouse over it to reveal its true path. The URL should appear at the bottom left corner of your web browser.

Again, check for a legitimate domain name in the URL.

http://www.domain.ca/

http://www.subdomain.domain.ca/

If you’re unsure about the validity of the email, don’t risk it! Before taking any further action, conduct a quick web search to look up the sender information.
Key clues to identify phishing emails
  • The sender’s email domain is invalid: contact@g0uv.ca
  • The message is not bilingual
  • There were spelling and grammatical errors in the text
  • The email urges you to take immediate action or else something will occur
  • Hovering over the link (without clicking) reveals a different URL: www.g0uv.com/private
  • The greeting was generic or absent
  • No contact information was provided
  • The email requests that you to send personal information
